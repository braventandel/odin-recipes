Chicken Biryani

About Chicken Biryani

Chicken Biryani is a majestic and aromatic dish that stands as a centerpiece of festive Indian dining. It is a layered delicacy where marinated chicken and basmati rice are cooked separately before being assembled and slow-cooked together in a sealed pot, a technique known as "dum." This process allows the steam to infuse the rice with the complex flavors of saffron, caramelized onions, and whole spices, resulting in a fragrant and hearty one-pot meal.

INGREDIENTS:

Chicken Marinade

  • 500g chicken (bone-in pieces)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Mint and coriander leaves (chopped)

Rice

  • 2 cups Basmati rice (soaked for 30 mins)
  • Whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 4 cloves, 4 green cardamoms, 1-inch cinnamon stick.
  • Salt

Layering/Assembly

  • 2 large onions (sliced thinly and fried until brown - "Birista")
  • 1/2 cup milk with a pinch of saffron threads soaked in it
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
  • Fresh mint and coriander leaves

STEPS:

  1. Marinate Chicken: Mix chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, spices, salt, and herbs. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Cook Chicken: Heat oil/ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add the marinated chicken and cook over medium heat until the chicken is 80% cooked and the oil separates.
  3. Cook Rice: Boil water with salt and whole spices. Add soaked rice and cook until it is 70% done (the grain should still have a slight bite). Drain the water.
  4. Layering (Dum): In a heavy pot, spread the cooked chicken gravy at the bottom. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken.
  5. Garnish: Top the rice with fried onions (Birista), chopped mint, and coriander. Pour the saffron milk and a tablespoon of melted ghee over the top.
  6. Steam (Dum Cooking): Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid (you can seal the edges with dough or aluminum foil). Cook on very low heat for 15-20 minutes.
  7. Serve: Fluff the rice gently to mix the layers and serve hot with Raita.
