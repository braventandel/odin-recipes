Butter Chicken

About Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken is one of the most beloved dishes in Indian cuisine, famous for its velvety, rich texture. It consists of tender pieces of chicken that are first roasted in a tandoor (clay oven) or pan-fried, and then simmered in a luscious, mildly spiced tomato gravy. Finished with generous amounts of butter and fresh cream, the curry offers a perfect balance of tangy and sweet flavors, making it an ideal comfort food to pair with naan or rice.

INGREDIENTS:

Marinade

  • 500g boneless chicken (cubed)
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Gravy

  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 cups tomato puree
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (fresh cream)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Spices: 1 tsp red chili powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, 1/2 tsp cumin powder, 1/2 tsp garam masala.
  • Salt to taste

STEPS:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, mix all the marinade ingredients. Add the chicken cubes and coat well. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (overnight is better).
  2. Cook the Chicken: Heat a little oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Pan-fry the chicken pieces until browned and cooked through. Set aside.
  3. Prepare the Base: In the same pan, heat the butter and oil. Add onions and saut√© until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute.
  4. Make the Gravy: Add the tomato puree, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  5. Simmer: Add a splash of water if the mixture is too thick. Add the cooked chicken pieces back into the pan. Simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  6. Finish: Lower the heat. Stir in the heavy cream, sugar, and garam masala. Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and sprinkle it over the curry.
  7. Serve: Garnish with a swirl of cream and serve hot with Naan or Rice.
