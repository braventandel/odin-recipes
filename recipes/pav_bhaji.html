Pav Bhaji

About Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a vibrant and spicy street food classic originating from the bustling streets of Mumbai. The dish features "Bhaji," a thick and flavorful mash of mixed vegetables like potatoes, cauliflower, and peas, cooked with a special blend of spices on a large flat griddle. It is traditionally served piping hot, topped with a dollop of butter, fresh coriander, and lemon juice, alongside "Pav," soft buttery bread rolls toasted to perfection.

Ingredients:

Bhaji(Vegetable Mash)

  • 3 medium potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets (boiled and mashed)
  • 1/2 cup green peas (boiled and mashed)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 capsicum (green bell pepper, finely chopped)
  • 2 tbsp Pav Bhaji Masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon juice and coriander leaves for garnish

For Serving

  • 8-10 Pav (soft bread rolls)
  • Butter for toasting
  • Chopped onions and lemon wedges

STEPS:

  1. Prep Vegetables: Boil the potatoes, cauliflower, and peas until soft. Drain and mash them together using a potato masher.
  2. Sauté Base: Heat butter and oil in a large pan (tawa). Add chopped onions and ginger-garlic paste. Sauté until translucent.
  3. Add Veggies: Add the chopped capsicum and tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes turn soft and mushy.
  4. Spice it up: Add the Pav Bhaji Masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a minute.
  5. Combine: Add the mashed vegetable mixture to the pan. Mix everything thoroughly.
  6. Simmer and Mash: Add water to adjust the consistency (it should be thick but pourable). Use the masher to mash the veggies directly in the pan again to get a smooth texture. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  7. Finish: Squeeze lemon juice on top, add a generous dollop of butter, and garnish with coriander.
  8. Toast Pav: Slit the pav rolls horizontally. Heat a pan with butter and toast the pav on both sides.
  9. Serve: Serve the hot Bhaji with buttered Pav, chopped onions, and lemon wedges on the side.
